# CV Best Practices Reference

**Version:** 1.0  
**Used by:** `skills/profile_curation/SKILL.md`

These principles govern the structure and quality of the tailored CV produced by the Profile Curation skill.

---

## Structure

**One page maximum for < 7 years experience; two pages acceptable for 7+ years.**  
When in doubt, cut. Recruiters spend ~6 seconds on initial review. Every line must earn its place.

**Standard section order (adjust based on JD priority):**
1. Name + contact details (clean header — no photo, no date of birth)
2. Professional Summary / Profile (3–5 sentences)
3. Experience (reverse chronological — most recent first)
4. Skills (functional and technical, JD-relevant first)
5. Education
6. Certifications / Professional Development (only if relevant)

**Do not include:**
- Objective statements ("Seeking a challenging role where I can...")
- References ("References available on request") — assumed
- Headshots or personal photos
- Date of birth, marital status, or any personal demographic

---

## Professional Summary

- 3–5 sentences maximum
- Lead with the user's current role/seniority and most relevant dimension for this JD
- End with one forward-looking sentence that names the type of role being targeted
- Ground every claim in the source material — no aspirational claims
- Avoid: "results-driven", "strategic thinker", "innovative", "passionate" without specific evidence

**Pattern:**  
`[Seniority + field] with [X years] in [relevant domain]. [Specific achievement relevant to JD]. [Key capability #1] and [Key capability #2]. Seeking [role type] to [specific goal grounded in their history].`

---

## Experience Bullets

**Lead with an action verb. Always.**

Strong verbs for impact: Led, Designed, Built, Launched, Reduced, Increased, Negotiated, Managed, Delivered, Implemented, Scaled, Restructured, Drove, Secured, Streamlined

Weak verbs to avoid: Assisted, Helped, Worked on, Supported, Participated in, Was responsible for

**STAR pattern (Situation → Task → Action → Result):**  
Not every bullet needs all four, but every bullet needs at least Action + Result.

- **Weak:** "Responsible for customer onboarding process"
- **Better:** "Redesigned customer onboarding workflow, cutting time-to-first-value from 14 days to 5"
- **Best:** "Led cross-functional redesign of customer onboarding across Product, Engineering, and Success, reducing time-to-first-value from 14 to 5 days and lifting 90-day retention by 12%"

**Quantify wherever the source material contains numbers:**
- Revenue, cost, percentage, headcount, time saved, customers served, projects delivered
- Never invent or round up numbers
- If no metric exists, describe the scope: "across 3 markets", "for 200+ enterprise accounts"

**Bullet length:**
- Maximum 2 lines when printed
- Prefer 1 line — if it spills past 2 lines, split into two bullets or cut

**Per-role bullet count:**
- Current/most recent role: 4–5 bullets (most relevant to JD first)
- Prior roles: 2–3 bullets
- Roles > 10 years ago or clearly irrelevant: 1–2 bullets or omit

## Skills Section

**Format:** Flat list or 2-column, no skill meters or ratings (they are meaningless and space-wasteful).

**Categorise if > 8 skills:**
- Technical: specific tools, platforms, languages
- Functional: methodologies, frameworks, domain expertise
- Languages: spoken languages if relevant

**Ordering:** JD-required skills first, then supporting skills, then peripheral.

**Never list a skill not evidenced** in the source CV or LinkedIn. If the skill appears on LinkedIn but not CV, it can still be included — note it comes from LinkedIn in the rationale log.

---

## Education

- Institution name, degree, field of study, graduation year
- GPA only if > 3.5 and graduated within last 5 years
- Relevant coursework only if directly relevant to a JD must-have and not otherwise evidenced

---

## ATS Compatibility

The tailored CV HTML output must be ATS-friendly:
- Single-column layout (not multi-column — ATS parsers struggle with columns)
- No text boxes, tables (except simple education tables), or images
- Standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills — not creative names like "My Journey")
- Use the JD's vocabulary where it naturally fits — ATS systems keyword-match against the JD
- File format: export as both HTML (for preview) and PDF (for submission)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it hurts |
|---------|-------------|
| "Responsible for..." | Passive, implies obligation not achievement |
| Skills listed without any evidence in experience | ATS and humans both distrust unsubstantiated claims |
| Generic summary that could apply to any role | Misses the ATS match window and fails to hook the recruiter |
| Missing quantification when numbers are available in source | Leaves impact implicit when it could be explicit |
| Every role given equal weight | Obscures the candidate's career trajectory and relevance |
| Buzzwords without demonstration | "Strategic thinker" is meaningless without evidence of strategy |
| Including unrelated early-career roles in full | Wastes space that should showcase recent, relevant experience |
